Reflections

Reflection on my learning journey into the Core Skills covered in the Foundations curriculum

28 September 2023

Three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning

I learned that I used to thrive on the pressure of leaving tasks until the last minute, which was causing unnecessary stress. By changing my approach and tackling certain tasks earlier in the week, like finishing blogs on Mondays, I've realized that I can manage my time more efficiently and reduce stress levels. Recognized the value of seeking help when needed, and prioritized self-care without guilt.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values help us make choices, empathy lets us understand others and work well together in teams, and self-awareness makes us learn better and get better at coding

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

What surprised me the most about the core learning was that it was part of the program in the first place. It felt like we were being encouraged to handle HR stuff on our own. Since I was already familiar with most of it, it turned out to be a great opportunity to test my self-awareness and practice self-talk when I wasn't fully confident in the subject.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

The most challenging part of the core learning, hands down, was the time it took to write those blogs. I was eager to dive into researching and learning about core skills and putting them into practice, but writing was a struggle. That's why I made a change and started tackling the blogs first so I could dedicate more time to the technical aspects

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I think it's because technical skills alone don't make a great developer. They want us to be well-rounded, not just coding machines. These core learning aspects, like communication, teamwork, and self-awareness, make us better collaborators, problem solvers, and professionals in the real world. So, it's about building not just coding skills but the whole package.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead?

I've come to appreciate it. It's not a waste of time. These skills, like communication and self-awareness, actually make me a better person and better developer. Plus, they're super valuable in the real world.
