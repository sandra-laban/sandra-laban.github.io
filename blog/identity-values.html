Identity, values, and strengths

This is reflection on my core personal values, and my strengths and limitations.

3 September 2023

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

When I notice someone looking lost or confused, perhaps a tourist trying to find their way, I don't hesitate to approach them to offer assistance. It's a small act of empathy, connecting with the confusion they might be feeling and offering a helping hand. Reflecting on such moments, I feel warmth in knowing that a simple act of understanding can sometimes mean a lot to someone in need.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

Growing up in Serbia taught me resilience and the importance of community. Moving to New Zealand enriched these values, introducing me to diverse cultures and a deep respect for nature. This blend of experiences has crafted my identity, encouraging empathy, harmony, and environmental consciousness in my daily life.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

My adaptability, honed from living in various countries, will help me adjust to different learning settings and work well with diverse groups. My resilience, built through personal experiences, will aid in overcoming challenges with a positive mindset. Leveraging my empathy will foster open and respectful communication with peers. I am confident that these strengths will guide me successfully through my learning journey.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey

One limitation I recognise in myself is my tendency towards perfectionism. While this drives me to produce high-quality work, it can sometimes lead to spending too much time on a task, trying to get it "just right." This perfectionism could potentially slow down my learning pace, especially when faced with complex subjects that present a steep learning curve, like learning JavaScript and JavaScript Frameworks, which is entirely new to me.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

At my job as a web designer, I had issues with a copywriter who was always late with their work, making it really hard to meet deadlines. They would give me the text for the website just a day before the project was due, leaving no time to properly test everything and make necessary changes.

At the time, I tried to fix this by often reminding them of the deadlines and starting on other parts of the website that didn't need the text. But this didn't work well, and we ended up rushing at the end, which affected the quality of our work.

Looking back, a better approach would have been to have a meeting at the start to plan out when each part of the project would be done, with clear deadlines for both of us. Next time, I'd set up a meeting at the beginning to make a clear plan and have regular check-ins to see how things are going. This way, everyone knows what they need to do and when, helping us work together more smoothly and avoid last-minute rushes.
